How These Goals Were Set

May goals were derived from April actual performance using a tiered improvement framework — not arbitrary targets. Each goal reflects what is realistically achievable with focused scheduling discipline in a single month.

  • Top performers (TPPH ≥ 6.75): +0.10 — maintain excellence, small stretch to protect position
  • Strong performers (6.25–6.74): +0.15 — push toward the top tier
  • Near average (5.85–6.24): +0.20 to +0.25 — scheduling efficiency focus, especially where labor % exceeds 21%
  • Below average (5.25–5.84): +0.30 to +0.35 — scheduling discipline required, reduce hours over need
  • Significantly below average (<5.25): +0.40 — priority intervention, high labor % and over-scheduling driving results

Important note on understaffing: Locations running short on hours are not automatically performing well. 24343 N. Veterans leads the portfolio in TPPH at 6.95 but is 668 hours understaffed — their May goal is reduced to 6.65, below their April actual — a significant service risk that must be addressed in May. 33945 Lafayette is similarly understaffed at -183 hours — their goal is also reduced to 5.99, below April actual. Adding proper staffing will temporarily reduce TPPH before it rebounds. Short hours can mask crew gaps that hurt OEPE, guest experience, and ultimately sales.

Key lever for all locations: The portfolio ran 2,979 overtime hours in April. Every hour of OT reduced is a direct TPPH improvement. Locations with the highest OT (02066 at 252 hrs, 02435 at 222 hrs, 05031 at 184 hrs) should make OT reduction their primary May focus.
