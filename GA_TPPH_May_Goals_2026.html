How These Goals Were Set & What Ideal TPPH Means

Ideal TPPH shows what each location's TPPH would be if their hours worked were adjusted to exactly match their staffing need (zero over or under). It is calculated as: Actual Guest Count ÷ (Actual Hours ± Act vs Need). Green = location would improve with correct staffing. Red = TPPH was inflated by understaffing and will drop when properly staffed.

May goals were set using a tiered improvement framework:

  • Top performers (TPPH ≥ 6.75): +0.10 — maintain, protect
  • Strong performers (6.25–6.74): +0.15 — push toward top tier
  • Near average (5.85–6.24): +0.20 to +0.25 — scheduling efficiency
  • Below average (5.25–5.84): +0.30 to +0.35 — scheduling discipline
  • Significantly below (<5.25): +0.45 — priority intervention
  • Understaffed locations: Goal reduced below April actual — adding staff will temporarily lower TPPH before it rebounds

Key lever: The portfolio ran 2,979 overtime hours in April. Every hour of OT reduced is a direct TPPH improvement. Locations with the highest OT (02066 at 252 hrs, 02435 at 222 hrs) should make OT reduction their primary May focus.

The Multiplier Commitment

The distance between #1 and #20 is not talent — it is knowledge, standards, and support. The Ideal TPPH column tells us that for several locations, the single biggest lever is not coaching their GM harder — it is fixing the scheduling. Get the hours right first, then build the TPPH habits that sustain it.
